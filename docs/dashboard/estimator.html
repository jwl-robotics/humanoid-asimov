02 ACT 1 · THE BACKBONE — LEG-ODOMETRY DEAD RECKONING scripts/run_estimator.py · offline replay over the full 8 s log

Leg odometry is run open-loop (no filter yet) to validate the measurement backbone before any fusion. Attitude R is supplied two ways: true attitude from the GT quaternion — isolating the translational drift floor of the anchor + FK chain itself — and gyro-integrated attitude (R ← R·exp(ω̂ dt) from the noisy gyro) — what dead reckoning really looks like with no filter. ⚑ the insight — translation is already drift-free; attitude is the whole game — with true attitude the error is a bounded sawtooth: it creeps ≈ 0.4 cm during a stance (foot micro-slip) and resets on every re-anchor — after 5.8 m it has accumulated essentially nothing (0.03 % drift). Swap in gyro-integrated attitude and the same pipeline climbs near-linearly to 3.2 cm (0.54 %) — every millimetre of that gap is attitude error leaking through the kinematic chain. That is the error budget the ESKF plays for: accelerometer gravity levels roll/pitch and contact makes bg observable — but yaw about gravity stays unobservable from IMU + contact alone, a floor the arc below runs straight into. The measurement backbone is validated — act 2 fuses it.

03 ACTS 2–5 · FUSE → FAIL → DIAGNOSE → FIX scripts/run_estimator.py · scripts/run_nees.py (M=10 seeds) · docs/STAGE1_ESKF_REVIEW.md

The ESKF landed in four beats. It ran with verified-correct math — and still drifted more than raw dead reckoning. A NEES/NIS consistency harness said why that smelled: the covariance was lying. The trail led to a contact assumption that is almost — but not exactly — true, and the fix was to make the filter estimate the lie.

FD-VERIFIEDDRIFT 0.73 %

But drift lands at 0.73 %, behind the 0.54 % dead reckoning it should beat: the ESKF fuses at the velocity level (position = ∫v̂), so any contact systematic random-walks the position.
correct math ≠ consistent filter — so interrogate the covariance, not just the error

ACT 4 · DIAGNOSE⌖ FOUND

THE PLANTED FOOT ISN'T STILL

CONTACT GEOMETRY0.61 m LEVER
the stance ankle site sits ~9 mm above ground and the foot rolls about a moving contact point — its true velocity is a systematic ~[−4, ±3, −7] mm/s, not zero. Via the 0.61 m lever arm it leaks into the gyro bias — bg y corrupted by +2.53 mrad/s (sign-flipped!) — and drives a hidden ~4 cm z drift.
tuning can't absorb a bias — matching Q to the injected noise only makes the covariance optimistic (a tight P can't explain a biased residual). fix the model, not the tuning.

ESTIMATE THE SLIP ONLINE

+3 STATES → 18-DIMDRIFT 0.32 %

the filter now estimates the systematic instead of eating it into bg

⚠ honest residuals — the slip-bias fix buys the point estimate, not the covariance — drift and bg get much better, but the reported uncertainty is optimistic: the contact NIS runs hot (mean ~6.3, median ~1.2 — not the ~3 a white dof-3 residual gives) and its lag-1 autocorrelation is +0.85: the within-stance error is correlated (the contact point rolls heel→toe through every stance) and the lateral slip flips sign per foot. A single shared bc can't track that — and a per-foot bc was tried and diverges: each foot is observed only ~half the time, so its fore-aft component aliases with base velocity. A diagonal-Q EKF fundamentally cannot model colored noise, and no Q/R tuning whitens it: matching Q to the injected noise instead drives the covariance optimistic across the board (per-block ANEES — velocity, tilt and both bias blocks all out of band; lumped 15-dof ANEES ~615). The principled fix is structural: a right-invariant EKF with foot-position contact states — position-level contact + trajectory-independent Jacobians → a whitened residual and an honest covariance. That is Stage 1.5 — now parked: Stage 2 went first because the drift was yaw-limited, and its vision update is gauge-safe by construction, adding no spurious information — the contact residual is carried, untouched.
⚑ the through-line — horizontal drift is yaw-limited — attitude error explains 85 % of the ESKF's translation error (corr 0.998); final yaw error 6.5 mrad ≈ raw gyro integration's 6.8 mrad; and bg z is provably unlearnable here by any filter — yaw-about-gravity + absolute position are the classic unobservable subspace of IMU + contact. The remaining drift is not a bug to tune away; it is missing information. That is exactly what Stage 2 — monocular VIO added: the head camera measures the yaw drift, and the missing states snapped in. Landed — on a 24 s closed loop with 360° of yaw, over 5 seeds: VIO ~0.15 % vs ESKF ~0.49 % median, and bg z — unlearnable on this page — pinned to ≤0.42 mrad/s on every seed (median 0.19, vs ~1.7 blind) ▸ the stage-2 deep dive
